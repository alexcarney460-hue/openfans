## 3. Valuation Framework

### Method A: Comparable Stage Discount (Top-Down)

Starting from OnlyFans' market position and discounting for stage:

| Factor | Discount | Rationale |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| No revenue | -99.5% | Pre-revenue vs $1.41B net revenue |
| No users/creators | -95% | Network effects = zero; marketplace is empty |
| No brand recognition | -90% | OnlyFans is a household name |
| Unproven unit economics | -80% | 5% take rate is aggressive, unvalidated |
| Single founder / no team | -70% | No org, no ops, no support |
| Product exists (MVP) | +small premium | Functional product, not just an idea |
| Crypto/Solana angle | +small premium | Differentiated payments, instant settlement |
| Better creator economics | +small premium | 95% payout is a genuine moat if it scales |

OnlyFans at $5.5B midpoint, discounted ~99.9% for all the above = **$2-5M implied value** — which aligns with typical pre-seed range.

### Method B: Stage-Based Market Comps (Bottom-Up)

Current market data for startup valuations (Q3 2025 - Q1 2026):

| Stage | Typical Valuation | Typical Raise |
|-------|-------------------|---------------|
| Pre-Seed (idea + MVP) | $5-10M | $250K-$1M |
| Seed (early traction) | $8-20M | $2-4M |
| Series A (proven PMF) | $30-80M | $8-15M |

OpenFans sits at **late pre-seed / early seed** — MVP is built and deployed, but zero traction.

**Pre-seed median (Q3 2025): $7.7M**

Creator economy premiums exist but are moderated by:
- Market saturation (many OnlyFans competitors have failed: Fansly, FanCentro, Patreon-adjacent)
- Chicken-and-egg marketplace problem (need creators to get fans, need fans to get creators)
- Regulatory risk (payment processors, adult content compliance)

### What Investors Will Ask
1. **How do you solve cold-start?** — Founder Program, creator-first economics, affiliate referral program
2. **Can you survive on 5% take rate?** — Show path to scale; at OnlyFans GMV ($7B), 5% = $350M net revenue
3. **Why won't OnlyFans just lower their fee?** — They're optimizing for profit extraction ($497M dividends), not creator welfare. Institutional inertia.
4. **Adult content compliance?** — Need clear answer on payment processing, content moderation, legal structure
5. **Why Solana?** — Instant settlement, <$0.01 transaction fees, no chargebacks, global by default

## 8. The Bull Case (Why This Could Be Big)

OnlyFans takes 20% — that's $1.44B/year extracted from creators. OpenFans at 5% returns $1.08B of that back to creators. That's a **$1 billion annual value proposition** to the creator base.

If OpenFans captured just **1% of OnlyFans' GMV** ($72M), at a 5% take rate that's $3.6M net revenue — enough to be a profitable, growing business valued at $30-50M.

At **5% of OnlyFans' GMV** ($360M), net revenue hits $18M — solidly Series B territory at $150-250M valuation.

The TAM isn't speculative. OnlyFans proved it. The question is whether OpenFans can peel off even a small slice with better economics.
